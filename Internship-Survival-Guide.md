## üôè Section 1.3: Indian Office Politics - Ego Management

### Rule 1: Senior Ko Credit Do (Even If You Did 100% Work)

**Scenario:** You fixed a tough bug, manager asks in meeting "kaun kiya?"

| ‚ùå DON'T Say | ‚úÖ DO Say |
|-------------|----------|
| "Maine kiya!" | "Sir, [Senior name] ne guide kiya tha, maine implement kiya" |

**Why?**
- Senior feels respected ‚Üí Will actually help you next time
- You still get mentioned
- No one's ego hurt
- You learn to play the long game

### Rule 2: Never Correct Senior Publicly

**Scenario:** Senior says something technically wrong in meeting.

| ‚ùå DON'T Do | ‚úÖ DO This |
|------------|----------|
| "Actually sir, that's not how React hooks work..." | Wait. After meeting, privately: "Sir, woh ek doubt tha, can you explain this part?" |

Let them "realize" the correct answer. They save face, you stay safe.

### Rule 3: Hierarchy Words Matter

| Person | How to Address |
|--------|---------------|
| Manager | Sir/Ma'am ALWAYS |
| Seniors (2-3 yrs ahead) | Start formal, observe their vibe |
| Direct peers | First name is fine |

**Pro Tip:** First 2 weeks, watch how others address seniors. Copy that.

### Rule 4: Chai Break Politics

> "Office politics doesn't happen in meetings. It happens in chai breaks."

- Eat lunch with team when invited
- Join chai breaks
- Don't wear earphones all day (seems unapproachable)
- Attend team outings

## üõ°Ô∏è Section 1.6: Protecting Yourself

### The 5 Survival Rules

1. **Trust, but verify** - Document important conversations
2. **Help everyone, but make it visible** - CC/loop manager on big tasks
3. **Never badmouth anyone** - Even if someone takes your credit
4. **Build allies** - Be genuinely helpful to 2-3 people
5. **Under-promise, over-deliver** - Say Thursday, deliver Wednesday

### When Senior Dumps Their Work on You

**Reality:** Common. "Yeh chhota kaam hai, tu kar de"

**Strategy:**
- If learning opportunity ‚Üí Do it happily
- If repeated grunt work ‚Üí "Sir, abhi [actual task] pe hu, manager ne priority bola. Baad me help karu?"

You're not saying NO - you're saying you have work from manager.

## ü´ì Section 2.3: Your Dough Plan - Important Tips

### What You Said:
> "I will dough flour for roti and parathas, keep in fridge, use portion when needed"

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Reality Check:

| Storage Duration | Dough Condition |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1-2 days | Good, usable |
| 3-4 days | Gets dry, hard, sour smell |
| 5+ days | Not recommended |

### Better Approach:

**Option A:** Make dough every alternate day (30 mins every 2 days)

**Option B:** Buy ready rotis from local shop (many shops sell fresh ones)

**Option C:** Make larger batch but freeze portions
- Divide dough into balls
- Wrap each in plastic wrap
- Freeze
- Take out night before, thaw in fridge

### For Paratha:
- Make aloo stuffing for 3-4 days ‚Üí Keep in fridge
- Stuffing lasts longer than dough

## üç≤ Section 2.5: Quick Recipes You Know

### Gutke Aloo (Roasted Potato)
```
Time: 15 mins

1. Boil potatoes (can do night before)
2. Peel and cut into cubes
3. Heat oil in pan
4. Add cumin seeds (jeera)
5. Add potato cubes
6. Add salt, red chilli, turmeric
7. Roast on medium heat till crispy
8. Done!
```

### Dahi Chawal (Curd Rice)
```
Time: 5 mins (if rice is pre-made)

1. Take leftover rice (or cook fresh)
2. Let it cool
3. Mix with curd
4. Add salt
5. Optional: Tadka with mustard seeds, curry leaves
6. Keep in fridge overnight
7. Pack for lunch next day
```

### Simple Dal
```
Time: 20-25 mins

1. Wash dal (toor/moong)
2. Pressure cook with water, turmeric, salt (3 whistles)
3. In separate pan: tadka with ghee, jeera, hing, red chilli
4. Pour tadka over dal
5. This lasts 3-4 days in fridge
```
